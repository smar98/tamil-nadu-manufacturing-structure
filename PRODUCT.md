# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Three audiences, one page, stated by the author directly. Primary: policy-adjacent readers —
a senior Tamil Nadu bureaucrat (Additional Secretary to the CM, Industries) and Harvard
faculty — reading on a laptop, skeptical by profession, who will check numbers against
sources they know. Secondary: laypeople with zero background in Indian statistics who must
be able to follow the entire story without being talked down to. The author (an MPP student
and researcher) sends the link cold; the page carries the whole first impression with no one
present to explain it.

## Product Purpose

A scroll-driven data story answering: how is Tamil Nadu's manufacturing employment
distributed, and how do value addition, pay and job quality compare with peer states?
Success = a reader of any of the three kinds finishes the scroll understanding the paradox
(most factory jobs in India, near-lowest value per worker), what the evidence rules out
(missing middle; industry mix as the main story), what it finds (within-industry gap;
largest labour share; split job-quality record), and what current policy does and does not
address — and comes away trusting the work enough to cite it or request the underlying
research.

## Positioning

The only account of Tamil Nadu manufacturing where every number on screen reconciles against
the government's own published tables — and says so.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: read to the end (the page IS the product); secondary: open the methods
  drawer / GitHub repository for the full research trail.
- The line a visitor remembers: "India's factory state — the most factory workers, nearly
  the least value per worker, and the workers' largest share of it."
- Belief ladder: (1) this person measured carefully → (2) the famous diagnosis is tested,
  not assumed → (3) the real gap survives the fair test → (4) policy doesn't currently aim
  at it → (5) I should take this author seriously.
- Proof on hand: 28 built-in reconciliation gates against published tables; an external
  validation register (matches to ≤2%, mostly ≪1%); exact-to-the-rupee reproductions cited
  inline. Proof is woven into the narrative, not a logo strip.

## Brand Personality

Measured, grounded, exacting. The voice of a field brief written by someone who did the
arithmetic themselves: plain declarative sentences, numbers stated with their limits,
confidence without salesmanship. Emotions to evoke: trust first, then quiet surprise at the
paradox, then respect for the honesty of the limits.

## Anti-references

Not a SaaS landing page (no hero-metric template, no gradient anything). Not a Tableau-style
dashboard of disconnected widgets. Not the 2026 AI-editorial default (cream page, italic
display serif, mono uppercase eyebrows on every section, numbered section markers). Not
India-flag or temple-poster palette cliché. Not breathless data-journalism ("SHOCKING gap")
— the register is a serious brief, not a viral scroller.

## Design Principles

1. The story earns the scroll: one idea per viewport, each section ends by creating the next
   section's question.
2. Three readers, three layers: plain prose → exhibit with caveat → "how we know" in full
   technical depth. Nothing dumbed down, nothing hidden.
3. Numbers carry their provenance: sample counts, survey universe, suppression marks and
   source citations sit beside the chart, not in a footnote no one finds.
4. Honesty is the aesthetic: suppressed cells, uncertainty and unknowns are designed
   elements, not omissions.
5. Craft signals credibility: to this audience, typographic and chart precision is evidence
   of statistical precision.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body contrast ≥4.5:1 (charts ≥3:1 for graphical objects); full keyboard paths
for all disclosure and exhibit controls; `prefers-reduced-motion` honored with crossfades;
charts carry text alternatives and direct labels (no color-only encodings — TN vs peers also
distinguished by weight/shape); content readable with JS off (steps render stacked).
