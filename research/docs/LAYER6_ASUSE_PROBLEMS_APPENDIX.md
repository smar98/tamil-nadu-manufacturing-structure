# Layer 6 optional exploratory appendix: ASUSE reported problems

This is the optional exploratory appendix named in `HANDOFF.md` section 6
("ASUSE can show associations between reported problems and size, age,
registration, ICT, assets, loans and GVA per worker. These are
hypothesis-generating correlations, not binding-constraint estimates.") It is
not a change to the Layer 1-5 estimand and does not touch the frontend, the
public payload, or `build_manufacturing_structure.py`.

Generated by `research/scripts/build_asuse_problems_appendix.py` into
`research/derived/asuse_problems_appendix.json`. The script reads only
`research/data/raw/ASUSE_DATA_2023_24_CSV.zip` and never prints or exports
respondent-level records or design identifiers.

## Locked specification

**Universe:** Tamil Nadu ASUSE 2023-24 manufacturing establishments (NIC
divisions 10-33). Weight = `mlt / 100`.

**Outputs:**

1. For each reported-problem category: weighted share of establishments
   reporting it — overall, by establishment type (OAE/HWE), and by size group
   (1-9, 10-49, 50-249, 250+ on reported total workers).
2. For each problem category: weighted GVA per worker (ratio of weighted
   totals, market establishments with valid GVA only, consistent with Layer
   3) among establishments reporting vs not reporting the problem.
3. For each problem category, among reporters vs non-reporters: weighted
   share with any registration, share using ICT/internet, share with an
   outstanding loan, and weighted mean establishment age.

**Disclosure rule** (the Layer 3 outcome-cell rule, applied to every
published cell): suppress when unweighted sample count < 10, or one
observation contributes > 70% of the weighted denominator, or > 70% of the
absolute weighted numerator. Suppressed values are `null`, never zero. Every
published cell carries its unweighted sample count. If a problem category's
reporter and non-reporter cells would allow reconstructing a suppressed
sibling by residual (the fixed universe total for that outcome is public
elsewhere in this project, e.g. Layer 3's Tamil Nadu GVA-per-worker figure),
both sides are nulled together.

## Exact field codes used

All fields are read from ASUSE Level 02 ("Block 2: particulars of operation
and background information") unless noted, verified against
`research/data/raw/asuse_2023_24_vol2.txt` lines 1780-1849 and
`research/data/raw/asuse_2023_24_report.txt` lines 39797-39960 (block C1-C4
code structures and questionnaire items).

| Dimension | CSV column | Questionnaire item | Metadata label | Codes used |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Reported problem (yes/no gate) | `problems_faced` | item 224 | "did the establishment face any problem in its operation during the last 365 days?" | yes-1, no-2 |
| Most severe problem | `most_severe_problem` | item 225 | "nature of problems faced ... most severe problem" | 1-9, see category table below |
| Next severe problem | `next_severe_problem` | item 226 | "nature of problems faced ... next severe problem" | 1-9, same code list |
| Registration | `registered` | item 227 | "whether registered under any act/authority?" | yes-1, no-2 |
| ICT/internet use | `used_internet` | item 223 | "did the establishment use internet during the last 365 days?" | yes-1, no-2 |
| Establishment age | `year_init_operation` | item 215 | "year of initial operation under the current owner at the present location" | calendar year, 1947-2024 in the extract |
| Establishment type | `est_type` | item 213 | "establishment type during the reference period" | hired-worker-1, own-account-2 |
| Total workers (size band) | `total_workers`, Level 09 item 789 | item 789 | Block 8 total workers | numeric |
| Outstanding loan | `amount_outstanding`, Level 12 (Block 11) item `1119` | item 1119 | "total (1101 to 1113)" of "[11] loan outstanding as on last date of the reference period" | Rs., total across all loan sources |

An establishment is counted as **reporting** problem category *c* if
`most_severe_problem == c` or `next_severe_problem == c` (an establishment
may report up to two problems, in order of severity). **Non-reporting**
covers everyone else in the universe for that category, including
establishments with `problems_faced == 2` (no problem at all) and
establishments that reported a different problem code.

### GVA per worker (deliverable 2)

Reuses the Layer 3 ASUSE definition exactly (`research/docs/LAYER3_FIELD_MAP.md`):
annual GVA (Level 08 item 769, annualized via Level 03 reference-period
fields `ref_period_type`, `days_closed_govt_order`, `months_in_accounting_period`)
divided by Level 09 item 789 total workers, restricted to market
establishments (`non_profit != 1` and `major_nic_5dig != 70100`) with
non-null GVA.

### Field substitution: establishment age

Item 214 (`years_of_operation`) is a banded categorical field (less than one
year-1, one to three years-2, more than three years-3, not known-4) and
cannot support a continuous weighted mean. Item 215
(`year_init_operation`) reports an actual calendar year and is used instead:
`age_years = 2024 - year_init_operation`, a fixed single reference year
across all establishments (an approximation; the true reference date varies
by sampled unit within the 2023-24 survey round). This substitution is
recorded here rather than left implicit.

### Field considered and omitted: Level 13 (Block 11.1)

Level 13 item numbers 1121/1122/1129 ("[11.1] amount of loan advanced by
financial establishment") record loans a financial-intermediation
establishment **advances to others**, gated on a separate item (258), not
the establishment's own borrowing. This is a different concept from "has an
outstanding loan" and is not used for the loan dimension in deliverable 3.
Only Level 12 (Block 11) item 1119, the establishment's own total
outstanding-loan balance, is used.

## Mandatory interpretation

These are **cross-sectional associations**, not binding-constraint
estimates. Reverse causation and selection are plausible explanations for
every pattern above: for example, more productive or more formal
establishments may be the ones with enough scale to seek finance, hire
staff, or notice a skills gap, so an establishment reporting a "problem" is
frequently the more capable establishment, not the more constrained one.

The strongest permitted wording is: **"establishments reporting X differ
from establishments not reporting X on Y."** Never: "X constrains growth,"
"establishments are being held back by X," or any causal or policy-binding
phrasing.

Concretely, in this data: establishments reporting non-availability of
skilled labour, non-availability of labour, or lack of technological
upgrade are far more likely to be registered, use ICT, and hold an
outstanding loan than establishments that do not report those problems, and
they show higher GVA per worker, not lower. Read at face value this is the
opposite of a "binding constraint" story — it is consistent with these
being the more formalized, more productive establishments, for whom growth
has made labour and technology gaps newly visible (a selection/reverse-
causation account), not evidence that labour or technology access is
holding back weaker establishments. The "others" category shows a flatter
or mildly reversed pattern (slightly lower GVA per worker and ICT use among
reporters), and "lack of connectivity to market" is the only category where
reporters have lower ICT use than non-reporters, again with no causal
reading available from this cross-section.

No number in this appendix should be cited as evidence that any single
factor constrains Tamil Nadu manufacturing growth.
