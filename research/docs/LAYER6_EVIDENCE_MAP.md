# Layer 6 evidence map: findings to policy instruments

Authoritative Layer 6 document. It maps demonstrated Layers 1–5 findings to verified current
policy instruments, states what the government reports, and states what remains unknown. It
makes no causal claim and does not assert actual official demand for this analysis.

Companion files: `LAYER6_FINDINGS_LEDGER.md` (full laundry list of findings, with numbers) and
`LAYER6_POLICY_EVIDENCE_NOTES.md` (verified quotations, page references, verification log).

## Evidence ladder used throughout

1. **Stated objective** — what an official document explicitly says;
2. **Instrument** — subsidy, loan, park, facility or programme;
3. **Output** — money disbursed, recipients, facilities built;
4. **Outcome** — employment, earnings, formalisation, GVA, coverage levels;
5. **Impact** — what happened because of the policy relative to a counterfactual.

Documents used here establish levels 1–3. The project's survey estimates describe some
level-4 outcomes. Nothing in v1 establishes level 5, and no wording may imply it.

## Historical objectives and the current policy gap

Tamil Nadu Industrial Policy 2021 (printed p.4) set: 15% annual manufacturing growth during
the policy term; ₹10 lakh crore investment 2020–2025; 20 lakh employment opportunities by
2025; manufacturing at 30% of GSVA by 2030. Its validity ended 31.03.2025 (printed p.3).

As of July 2026 there is **no verified successor or extension**: only sectoral policies
(Space 2025, Textile 2025–26, Toy 2025, Shipbuilding 2026, Circular Economy 2026) and a
June 2026 Governor's-address statement of intent to unveil a new industrial policy and an
Investor Promotion Commission, citing a "1.5 trillion dollar economy by 2036" aspiration.
Layer 6 therefore treats general manufacturing targets as a **policy gap**: current
instruments exist, but no current general target framework is verifiable. The 2030 GSVA
target may only be cited as a historical stated objective.

## Findings with no matching current instrument

F3 (labour share/pay levels) and F5 (earnings levels), and the contract/paid-leave components
of F4, map to no verified current stated objective. This is itself a Layer 6 result: the
state's verified current instruments target investment, category graduation, EPF-covered
hiring and sectoral development — not wage levels, labour share, written contracts or paid
leave.

## Two focused policy tests (v1)

1. **Scale-transition gap test (F1 × scale-up subsidy).** The state subsidizes category
   graduation; the descriptive evidence shows no unusually thin middle. Wording: statewide
   size scarcity is not an adequate justification by itself; justification may lie in
   productivity, formalisation, regional or sector-specific grounds the current data cannot
   test.
2. **20-worker threshold diagnostic (F6 × payroll subsidy).** Describe structure around the
   threshold and social-security coverage by enterprise size. Never call it an evaluation.

## Budget Speech 2025–26 evidence (verified 2026-07-13)

Current budget evidence that investment, parks, MSME lending and job creation remain active
concerns — all level-1/2 statements with announced outputs, no outcome or impact evidence:

- §107, p.49: "As the next step, the Government will launch and implement the 'Tamil Nadu
  Semiconductor Mission-2030' over the next five years with an estimated allocation of
  Rs.500 crore." Also a Semiconductor Fabless Lab in Chennai, ₹100 crore.
- §109, p.50: Tidel Park Hosur and mini-Tidel Virudhunagar, ₹400 crore, "employment for
  6,600 people."
- §111, pp.51–52: two footwear parks (Melur, Cuddalore), ₹250 crore, 10,000 + 10,000 jobs;
  engineering/fabrication park in Trichy, 250 acres, 5,000 jobs.
- §113, p.53: "Tamil Nadu Maritime Transport Manufacturing Policy 2025" — a new sectoral
  policy (shipbuilding), projected 30,000 jobs in Cuddalore and Thoothukudi. Sectoral, not a
  successor to the 2021 general Industrial Policy.
- §115, p.54: Industries, Investment Promotion and Commerce Department allocation ₹3,915
  crore.
- §116, p.54: MSME lending FY 2025–26 — banks to lend to 10 lakh MSMEs, outlay ₹2.5 lakh
  crore.
- §117, p.55: nine new SIDCO industrial estates, ₹366 crore, "17,500 employment
  opportunities."

Announced job numbers are projections attached to instruments (level 2), not measured
outcomes (level 4). The speech contains no extension of the 2021 Industrial Policy.

## Interpretation boundaries

- Instruments' existence never implies take-up, demand or impact.
- Adjusted Layer 5 gaps are descriptive composition controls; pairwise weights forbid
  league-table readings across comparators.
- Interviews are required before presenting any of this as answering an official decision
  need; until then the matrix links findings to documents, not to decisions.
- Suppressed cells stay suppressed; the appendix and matrix expose no suppressed estimate.
