# Red-Team Review

## 1. Does the project prove a "missing middle"?

**No.** The three displayed averages come from populations defined partly by
hiring and factory-coverage rules. They do not form a harmonized firm-size
histogram, and the surveys do not follow the same firms. The dashboard now
uses a neutral structural-snapshot title and treats "missing middle" only as a
hypothesis requiring common size bands, peer benchmarks and longitudinal data.

## 2. Does the 11.1x productivity ratio measure a registration effect?

**No.** Sector mix, capital intensity, firm age, selection, prices and labor
denominator differences can generate the gap. The ratio is descriptive and is
explicitly labeled as not being the return to registration.

## 3. Are own-account establishments policy failures?

**No.** Many may be viable livelihood activities with no desired factory growth
path. The policy section separates livelihood support from investigation of
growth-oriented firms.

## 4. Does the sector bubble chart identify constrained industries?

**No.** It measures employer incidence, productivity and establishment mass.
A low HWE share may reflect production technology, home-based work, demand or
preferences rather than a government-remediable barrier. The chart identifies
questions, not intervention targets.

## 5. Do registered-sector GVA levels prove technological capability?

**No.** GVA per person engaged is affected by capital, prices, market power and
industry composition. The registered-sector strip is a composition warning,
not a capability index.

## 6. Does PLFS validate ASUSE or ASI counts?

**No.** PLFS independently describes people, while ASUSE and ASI describe
establishments. PLFS supports only the broad observation that manufacturing
contains both wage work and self-employment.

## 7. Could public aggregates expose respondents?

The web payload contains no unit identifiers. ASI aggregate construction
suppresses cells with fewer than 10 sampled factories or one factory exceeding
70% of absolute weighted GVA. The final site uses only high-stability ASI
sectors with at least 100 sampled factories.

## Residual risks

1. ASI persons engaged and ASUSE workers are only approximately comparable.
2. Survey periods overlap but do not align exactly.
3. Detailed ASUSE sector uncertainty is not shown as confidence intervals.
4. ASI and ASUSE are not a complete, mutually exclusive partition under every
   coverage edge case.
5. Administrative records are needed to study transitions and policy exposure.

## Verdict

The project is defensible as a descriptive structural diagnostic and research
agenda. It is not a causal evaluation or proof that medium firms are missing.
